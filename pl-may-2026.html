02

Timeline of decisions

What we did at each step, and what it caused.

2026Mar 6–12
Broadened Meta targeting from five persona campaigns to a single Interest-based audience.
CPL held at ~48 PLN, same as the persona era. The new Interest-based baseline became our proven Meta channel — still our best performer today.
2026Apr 1
Scaled three Google experiments in parallel.
Performance Max with broad targeting, Search Generic budget tripled, and Google Lead Forms via Demand Gen. Assumption: broader targeting and easier signup would grow qualified volume.
2026Apr 14
Launched three Lookalike ad sets on Meta (1%, 1–3%, 3–10%) on top of the Interest-based baseline.
Assumption: lookalikes would let us scale qualified volume. Volume did grow — leads jumped to 211 in April, our highest month — but at 2–4× baseline CPL.

2026Apr 17
Launched Performance Max Dynamiczna as a controlled PMax variant.
A more targeted alternative to the broad PMax that was already underperforming. This became our best Google performer at 66 PLN CPL.
2026Apr 23
Paused Lookalike 3–10% — worst CPL at 180 PLN.
First experiment to be cut. The widest lookalike was ~4× our baseline CPL — no path to efficiency.
2026May 12
Paused Lookalike 1–3% (124 PLN CPL). Launched a duplicate ad set to force-deliver the Suzuki Swift creative.
Two cuts and one new experiment. The Suzuki Swift creative wasn't getting impressions in the main ad set, so we duplicated it to test whether the offer itself could pull. Result: 1 lead on 517 PLN total spend. The creative doesn't resonate.
2026May 13
Pulled all Google broad-targeting campaigns and stopped Google Lead Forms entirely.
Daily Meta impressions dropped from 13k+ to ~1k within 48 hours. Google Lead Forms confirmed as the largest single source of unqualified leads — 79 leads across April–May, only ~7 qualified.

2026Mid-May
Sales worked through the April backlog. Real May TAK% confirmed at 25%.
Through April the volume looked productive (211 leads, our highest month). Pre-qualification, the experiments read as working — we were planning against the Jan–Apr ~50% baseline. Once the backlog cleared, the picture was unmistakable. Cuts to underperformers were already underway.
2026May 26
Cleanup complete. Website channel recovered. Findings locked in.
May total spend down to ~10k PLN. Website Cost per TAK lead dropped from 574 PLN (April peak) to 261 PLN, back in the Feb–March range. Two channels validated as scale candidates; multiple experiments definitively retired.

04

What the experiments taught us

Six concrete findings. Decisions for June already taken.

Scale
Meta Interest-based baseline
56 PLN CPL · holds across the whole period
The single most reliable Meta channel we've run. The Interest-based audience scales without breaking efficiency — June budget should anchor here first.
Scale
Google Performance Max Dynamiczna
66 PLN CPL · 53 leads on 3,495 PLN
Our best Google performer by a wide margin. Currently 12% of Google spend — needs significantly more. Controlled targeting works on PMax; broad targeting does not.
Stop permanently
Google Lead Forms
9.7% TAK rate · 79 leads, ~7 qualified
One-click signup attracts noise, not intent. Children clicking through games. Blank-name submissions. Obvious mistakes. Known pattern for B2B — confirmed for our segment.
Stop
Meta Lookalike audiences
2–4× baseline CPL across all three tiers
Lookalikes scaled volume but at consistently worse economics. Wider lookalike = worse CPL with no quality offset. Not a path to growth for us.
Rebuild
Suzuki Swift creative
1 lead on 517 PLN across all ad sets
Force-delivery via duplicate ad set didn't change the outcome — the creative itself doesn't resonate with our audience. Input for the next creative round on Swift and Polo.
Validated
Website / Showroom channel
261 PLN per TAK lead in May · down from 574 PLN peak
Recovered to Feb–March range the moment the broad campaigns were cut. Higher-friction signup = higher-intent leads. Worth scaling alongside Meta Interest-based.
